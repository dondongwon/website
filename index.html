Dong Won Lee

I’m currently a Master’s in Machine Learning Student at Carnegie Mellon University. I recently completed my undergraduate degree at Carnegie Mellon, as well.

I am primarily interested in designing AI models to understand the relationship between what we see (vision) and what we hear (language) and implement these findings to interesting applications in education, human-robot-interaction, and robotics control.

Research

During my Bachelor’s and Master’s, I’ve primarily worked in the MultiComp Lab with Professor Louis-Phillipe Morency. I'm currently fascinated in learning fine-grained visual grounding between visual elements and language. Prior, I focused on developing models in generating nonverbal behavioral cues (gestures) conditioned on language to improve naturalness in human-robot interaction. I work with Dr. Hae Won Park at the MIT Media Lab to predict people’s engagement with an agent using graphical models. I am also collaborating with Professor Ruslan Salakhutinov to teach robots to learn optimal policies just with language.
